# 05 — Resident Hub

The Resident Hub is the digital version of the home inside its community. It must feel like a familiar mobile launcher: calm, readable, touch-friendly, and composed of mini apps.

## Home

- Greeting, community and unit identity
- Urgent actionable cards: guest waiting, package arrived, unpaid charge, reservation change
- App grid: My Unit, Finance, Guests, Services, Reservations, Cafe, Grocery, Laundry, Car Wash, Marketplace, Professionals, Property, Community
- Recent activity and global search
- Assistant entry point and notification preferences

## Key journeys

### Guest
Notification shows name, gate, host unit, time, and optional image. Actions: approve once, approve until a time, reject, message guard. The decision immediately updates the guard panel and audit log.

### Finance
The resident sees current charge, past balance, approved payments, pending receipts, and downloadable statement. A receipt upload enters accountant review; it does not silently settle the balance.

### Services
Select a service, describe the request, add photos, choose time, see price/estimate, submit, follow status, message provider, confirm completion, rate.

### Community economy
Users may activate a professional card, list lawful products or services, publish limited daily inventory, receive orders, and manage availability. Examples include teaching, consulting, home food, pickles, clothing, and a scheduled bread-buying service.

### Civic participation
Notices, surveys, formal votes, campaigns, suggestions, and tracked responses are available without mixing formal governance with casual chat.

## Accessibility

Large touch targets, Persian RTL, simple language, reduced motion, clear status colors with text labels, and an optional simplified mode for older users.
