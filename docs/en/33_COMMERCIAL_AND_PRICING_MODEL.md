# 33 — Commercial, Pricing, and Product Economics

## Model principle

The paying customer is primarily the community, management company, or developer. Residents are not forced into a separate subscription for essential core access. Final price follows pilot evidence and cost-to-serve measurement.

## Proposed packages

| Package | Fit | Includes | Price basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pilot | one block or 20–40 units | limited setup, training, operational core, pilot report | fixed time-bound fee |
| Core | small/medium community | unit, membership, notice, guest, package, ticket, basic booking | monthly minimum + active unit |
| Operations | large community | Core + management, contract, report, basic finance, channel | active unit + module |
| Platform | developer/multi-site operator | multi-tenant, branding, API, SLA, aggregate report | annual contract |

## Permitted revenue

- setup, data cleanup, and training
- community SaaS by active unit and module
- support/SLA and controlled customization
- transparent service/partner transaction fee by contract
- enterprise integration, custom report, and white label

Selling personal data, hidden advertising, resident-income promises, or guaranteed ROI are not Hamseen’s model.

## Pricing scenario formula

```text
MRR = base_platform_fee
    + active_units × per_unit_fee
    + enabled_paid_modules
    + support_tier
```

Use Conservative/Base/Growth scenarios. Figures remain placeholders until infrastructure, compensation, tax, support, and conversion inputs are sourced.

## Unit economics

| Metric | Formula |
|---|---|
| ARPA | monthly revenue / paying communities |
| Gross margin | (revenue - infrastructure - messaging - direct support) / revenue |
| CAC | sales and new deployment spend / new customer |
| Payback | CAC / monthly customer gross profit |
| Logo churn | lost communities / opening communities |
| NRR | ending recurring revenue including expansion/contraction / opening existing-customer revenue |
| Support cost/unit | direct support cost / active unit |

Numeric targets follow pilot data.

## Costs

- product engineering, design, security, QA
- hosting, database, storage, backup, monitoring
- SMS/Push/Bale/Telegram and AI providers
- onboarding, migration, training, support
- sales, contract, accounting, legal, insurance
- incident, recovery, and SLA reserve

## Sales path

Lead → discovery → role demo → data/process assessment → pilot proposal → contract/DPA → pilot → value report → expansion. Sell measurable friction reduction and trust, not a long feature list.

## Investment

Proposed use: product/engineering, security/infrastructure, pilot/support, enterprise sales, legal/operating reserve. No document promises guaranteed return or fabricated valuation.

## Open external decisions

Legal entity, tax, invoicing, gateway, message rates, data agreement, IP ownership, terms, and final price require legal/finance approval and market evidence.
